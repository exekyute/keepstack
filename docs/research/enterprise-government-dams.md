# Enterprise & Government DAM Landscape (research source)

Compiled June 2026 via live web research. Citation-backed source file feeding
`RESEARCH.md`. Recurring theme: almost no vendor holds a true product-level
FedRAMP authorization for the DAM itself, and Section 508 / WCAG conformance
is a near-universal blind spot. Those two gaps plus opaque pricing define
where an open challenger competes.

## The 15 platforms

1. **Orange Logic / Cortex** (SaaS/AWS). SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI, HIPAA, TX-RAMP, **VPAT for WCAG 2.2 / 508 / EN 301 549**; no FedRAMP. ~$60k/yr entry, not per-seat. Customers: UNICEF (100+ offices), Colonial Williamsburg, Magnum Photos, National Geographic, BBC. Killer: territory-level granular access + full audit, **OAIS digital preservation + WORM**, enterprise DRM, C2PA provenance. Weakness: steep learning curve, "overbearing," costly add-ons. [security/compliance](https://www.orangelogic.com/features/dam-security-compliance)
2. **MediaValet** (SaaS/Azure). SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001; inherits Azure FedRAMP High (no own ATO). **Unlimited users on every plan**, ~$6k/yr entry. Customers: City of Kawartha Lakes, US Naval Institute, Emory. Killer: Azure-native security, gov per-department portals, unlimited-user pricing. Weakness: dated UI, search "less intuitive" with AI. [security](https://www.mediavalet.com/why-mediavalet/security)
3. **Widen / Acquia DAM** (SaaS/AWS). ISO 27001, SOC 1/2. **Acquia Cloud is FedRAMP but the DAM is NOT covered.** Partial VPATs, "not yet fully AA." Customers: Univ of Kansas, Georgia, Penn. Killer: 200+ integrations, multi-instance DAM. Weakness: unreliable search, clunky UI, slow support. [trust](https://security.acquia.com/)
4. **Bynder** (SaaS/AWS, residency choice incl. US-only). Broadest ISO portfolio (27001/27018/22301); no FedRAMP, no VPAT. Customers: Spotify, Puma, KLM (weak gov footprint). Killer: most mature AI (NL search 100+ langs, similarity, OCR, face, speech-to-text), best-rated UX. Weakness: cost scales fast, lag on large libraries. [security](https://www.bynder.com/en/security/)
5. **Canto** (SaaS). ISO 27001; "monitors for SOC 2" (not a completed report); no FedRAMP/VPAT. **Strongest gov go-to-market via Carahsoft** (NASPO, TIPS, OMNIA, E&I). Killer: unlimited branded portals, AI visual search, SLED procurement channel. Weakness: clunky UI, lag on large files, weak facial recognition, cannot rename files in-platform. [Carahsoft partnership](https://www.carahsoft.com/news/canto-and-carahsoft-partner-to-deliver-digital-asset-management-solutions-to-government-and-education-markets-2025)
6. **NetX** (SaaS **and on-premise**). **Weakest certs** (no FedRAMP/SOC 2/ISO/508 published). Customers: Global Affairs Canada, UN Office of Counter-Terrorism, City of San Diego, The Met (1M+ assets), MoMA, Guggenheim, NGA. Killer: deep museum CMS integrations (Axiell, Gallery Systems), **IIIF support**, rights/download-justification workflows. Weakness: lag, limited AI auto-tagging, doc gaps. [customers](https://www.netx.net/customers)
7. **OpenText Media Management** (on-prem/cloud/hybrid; UK G-Cloud). **FedRAMP belongs to OpenText Cloud for Government (ECM/AppWorks), NOT OTMM.** 508 VPAT on request; Canada IRAP. Killer: retention policies applied to video/assets, AI rich-media search, deep ECM integration. Weakness: very complex to configure, consultant-dependent, bugs. [FedRAMP press](https://www.opentext.com/en-gb/about/press-releases/opentext-cloud-for-government-solution-achieves-fedramp-authorization)
8. **Adobe Experience Manager Assets** (cloud-first + on-prem). **The only true product-level FedRAMP Moderate (AEM Managed Services), plus ACR/VPAT vs WCAG 2.1/508.** Customers: US Census Bureau, NASA, State of Oklahoma, City of Sacramento. Killer: Sensei + Firefly (Smart Tags, OCR, **AI alt-text**), Content Credentials provenance, Dynamic Media, Creative Cloud. Weakness: premium cost, steep learning curve, SI-dependent. [FedRAMP Marketplace](https://www.fedramp.gov/marketplace/products/F1509037239/)
9. **Aprimo** (SaaS/Azure). SOC 2 Type II only; no ISO/FedRAMP/508 on trust page. Customers: US Dept of Homeland Security, Harvard, Washington State. Killer: regulated-industry workflow (MLR/compliance), expiration-triggered asset lockdown, agentic AI metadata. Weakness: steep setup, slow search, dashboard changes require paid support. [trust](https://trust.aprimo.com/compliance)
10. **Brandfolder (Smartsheet)** (SaaS/GCP). SOC 2 + ISO 27001; **FedRAMP/IL4 attach to separate Smartsheet Gov, NOT Brandfolder.** Killer: top ease-of-use, brand portals with partner upload, AI auto-tag taxonomy routing. Weakness: organizational overload, weak search, stagnant, no small-company tier. [security](https://help.smartsheet.com/articles/2482853-Brandfolder-Security)
11. **Cloudinary** (developer DAM, SaaS/AWS). **Transparent public pricing** (Free/$89/$224/Enterprise). SOC 2, ISO 27001; no real FedRAMP claim on own trust center; no VPAT. Killer: programmable media APIs/SDKs, on-the-fly transforms + CDN, AI media ops. Weakness: unpredictable cost, **account suspended on quota overage**, weak multi-user governance ("bolted on"). [trust](https://cloudinary.com/trust)
12. **Frontify** (SaaS/AWS, US or Frankfurt). ISO 27001, TISAX, Cyber Essentials, HIPAA; no own SOC 2, no FedRAMP/508. Customers: Microsoft, Uber, Lufthansa (no gov). Killer: brand-management suite (guidelines + portals), EU data residency. Weakness: clunky, weak API, MAU pricing, search degrades at scale. [security](https://www.frontify.com/en/security)
13. **Preservica** (digital preservation, SaaS + private cloud; UK G-Cloud). **Transparent low end** (free Starter, $310/mo Starter Plus). SOC 2, ISO 27001/9001, Cyber Essentials Plus, TX-RAMP, **VPAT WCAG/508 + ADA Title II**; OAIS (ISO 14721). Customers: **UK National Archives, ~20 US state archives, LA County, City of Boston, NYPL, Yale, the UN**. Killer: **Active Digital Preservation** (continuous format migration), tamper-proof chain of custody, **records retention via M365 labels**, **FOIA/public-records portal**. Weakness: thin review footprint, no FedRAMP, weaker as day-to-day distribution DAM. [trust center](https://preservica.com/trust-center)
14. **Axiell** (collections management, SaaS + on-prem; UK G-Cloud). ISO 27001; no SOC 2/FedRAMP. Real strength is **domain standards**: SPECTRUM, ISAD(G), DACS, RAD, RDA, MARC, Dublin Core, EAD, LIDO, CIDOC + OAI-PMH/SRU. Customers: Smithsonian NMNH, NHM London (13.7M records), AMNH, Te Papa, Van Gogh Museum; on Canada CHIN vendor list. Killer: deepest archive/museum standards, EMu at 10M+ scale, per-field RBAC, Linked Open Data. Weakness: steep learning curve, database-centric, migration burden; **collections mgmt not OAIS preservation**. [Collections Trust](https://collectionstrust.org.uk/software/axiell-collections/)
15. **Extensis Portfolio** (primarily on-prem/server). **No SOC 2/ISO/FedRAMP/508 for Portfolio.** Customers: Baylor, Columbia (evaluating), Teylers Museum (skews corporate/higher-ed). Killer: ease of use, fast visual search, NetPublish public portal with **VRA Core/Spectrum templates**, on-prem option. Weakness: scaling problems at 1M+, unreliable syncs, not a preservation/compliance platform. [research.com](https://research.com/software/reviews/extensis-portfolio)

## Why governments choose these (procurement drivers)
- **FedRAMP / DoD IL4-IL5** federal cloud authorization (only Adobe AEM holds a true product-level FedRAMP Moderate; others inherit, sibling-product, or lack it). State/local/edu fall back on TX-RAMP / StateRAMP and Carahsoft/NASPO/OMNIA/TIPS cooperative vehicles.
- **Accessibility (Section 508 / WCAG 2.x / EN 301 549)** with a VPAT/ACR. Only Orange Logic, Adobe, OpenText (on request), Preservica publish meaningful conformance.
- **Records management / retention schedules / legal hold** (OpenText, Aprimo, Preservica).
- **Audit trails / chain of custody** (Orange Logic, MediaValet, Aprimo, OpenText, Preservica).
- **Granular access control / classification** with SSO (Orange Logic to territory, Axiell to field, NetX with justification).
- **On-prem / air-gapped / data residency** (NetX, OpenText, Extensis, Axiell on-prem; Bynder/Frontify/Preservica/Cloudinary/Acquia residency choice).
- **Long-term digital preservation (OAIS / format migration)** (Preservica, Orange Logic).
- **FOIA / public-records + public discovery portals** (Preservica, NetX, Canto, Extensis NetPublish).
- **GLAM domain metadata standards** SPECTRUM/ISAD(G)/Dublin Core/EAD/MARC/LIDO/VRA Core/IIIF (Axiell, NetX, Extensis).
- **Procurement channels** GSA/SEWP/Carahsoft (US), G-Cloud (UK), CHIN (Canada).

## Gaps / openings for a free open-source tool
1. **Accessibility gap is near-universal** (most publish no 508/WCAG VPAT) yet it is a hard procurement requirement. WCAG 2.2 AA out of the box + AI alt-text beats the field on a checkbox they fail.
2. **Pricing is opaque and punitive** (quote-only, per-power-seat, MAU, credit metering that suspends accounts). Free + self-hostable + predictable wins outright.
3. **Self-hosting / data sovereignty without enterprise pricing** (air-gap for free is OSS home turf).
4. **Records retention / disposition is paywalled** into the most expensive tiers.
5. **Open metadata standards as a first-class citizen** (Dublin Core, EAD, MARC, LIDO, IIIF, OAI-PMH) instead of museum-only editions.
6. **Search quality is a universal complaint** (Acquia, Bynder, Canto, Brandfolder, Aprimo, Frontify); modern vector/semantic search is now cheap.
7. **Audit + granular RBAC + chain-of-custody checksums** match features incumbents reserve for top tiers.
8. **No vendor lock-in / open formats** (migration pain is a recurring incumbent failure).
9. **The "good enough" bar is lower than the price** (incumbents are complex, consultant-dependent, over-featured).

**What OSS likely cannot match short-term:** a true FedRAMP/IL4-IL5 ATO and Adobe-grade generative AI + Creative Cloud. So the realistic open wedge is **state/local/education, museums/archives/libraries, NGO/international** buyers where the binding constraints are cost, accessibility, open standards, data sovereignty, and retention, not a federal cloud ATO.
